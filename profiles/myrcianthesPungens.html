Myrcianthes pungens

Family:

Myrtaceae

Common Name / Native Name:

Guabiju, Guaviyu

Sun:

Full sun/ Part shade

Height

24-40 feet in it’s natural environment, certainly seems to grow quickly, and large in California

Width:

14- 20 feet, hedge capability

Foliage:

Bright red new growth to dark green, pointy and slightly wavy leaves

Flower:

White, large, myrtaceous flower, slightly musky fragrance

Soil Type / pH:

Prefers acidity but does very well in high pH soil

USDA Climate Zones:

9b+, with potential to 9a

Cold Hardiness:

Survived frosts to high 20’s for brief amounts of time, slight tip die back but rapid growth

Edibility:

Delicious fruit said to be reminiscent of jaboticaba, preferred eaten out of hand but great in preserves.

Age to Maturity

3-7 years
